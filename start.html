THE TRAP

The sunset paints the sky pink. I’m on a lakeside bench, book in hand, when Clara’s footsteps crunch closer. She holds a wildflower, her smile brighter than the fading light. “For you,” she says, our fingers brushing as I take it. My heart races. We sit, shoulders touching, the lake glowing. No words, just us, perfect in the quiet.

Suddently your phone buzzes. A new email:

URGENT: YOUR BANK ACCOUNT HAS BEEN LOCKED
Click here to verify your identity or lose access within 24 hours.

The sender looks legitimate. The logo is perfect. The fear is real.

CHOOSE YOUR FATE:

  1. [CLICK THE LINK] (It’s probably fine… right?)
  2. [IGNORE IT] (Better safe than sorry.)
  3. [LOG IN MANUALLY] (The slow, safe way.)

(Each choice leads to a different cybersecurity lesson—phishing, social engineering, or good security habits.)
